Gordon Andrews

Designing a Nation

The creative force behind Australia’s first decimal banknotes and beyond.

Featured Work

Australian Decimal Banknotes (1966) – Image of the $2 Note

Gordon Andrews designed Australia’s first decimal banknotes, introduced in 1966, creating a distinctly Australian visual identity. The $2 note features John Macarthur, a key figure in the wool industry, with intricate security patterns and vibrant colours. Andrews’ work combined artistic detail with anti-counterfeiting measures, shaping the future of Australian currency design.

“Gordon Andrews: A Designer’s Life” (1993) – Exhibition & Book

This retrospective exhibition, held at the Powerhouse Museum, celebrated the breadth of Gordon Andrews’ career in graphic, industrial, and exhibition design. The accompanying monograph, Gordon Andrews: A Designer’s Life, was published the same year by NSW University Press. The exhibition showcased his influential work, from furniture and urban wayfinding systems to his famous currency designs, cementing his role in shaping Australia’s modern visual identity.

Cameron Offices (1970s) – Architectural Interiors & Wayfinding

Designed in collaboration with architect John Andrews (no relation), the Cameron Offices in Canberra were an example of Brutalist architecture, where Gordon Andrews contributed to interior design and wayfinding systems. His work blended functionality with modernist aesthetics, integrating custom signage and design elements that complemented the building’s geometric, industrial structure. His approach to urban design helped enhance navigation and user experience in large-scale public spaces.
